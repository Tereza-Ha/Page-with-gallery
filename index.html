The Beskids

The Beskids or Beskid are a scenic range located in Central Europe, primarily spanning the borders of the Czech Republic, Slovakia, and Poland. Part of the Carpathian Mountains, the Beskydy are known for their lush forests, rolling hills, and charming villages. The region is a popular destination for hiking, skiing, and outdoor activities, offering diverse wildlife and rich cultural heritage.
Its highest peak, Lysá hora, is a well-known landmark in the Czech Republic. The Beskids also boast numerous natural reserves and are important for their biodiversity and traditional folklore.

The nature of the Beskids is a serene and diverse landscape filled with rich forests, flowing rivers, and peaceful meadows. The region is dominated by beech, spruce, and fir trees, creating a lush environment that supports a variety of wildlife, including deer, foxes, and even rare species like lynx and bears.
In the spring and summer, wildflowers blanket the hillsides, while autumn transforms the forests into a tapestry of vibrant colors. The clean mountain air and tranquil atmosphere make the Beskids a perfect escape for those seeking a connection with nature, offering an unspoiled, peaceful retreat amidst breathtaking natural beauty.

The most famous buildings on Pustevny are Libušín and Maměnka. Both of them were built in 1898 thanks to the efforts of the touristic club Pohorská jednota Radhošť. They are richly decorated log buildings, typical for the Valašsko region and Slavic architecture in general.
The statue of Radegast is a statue of the alleged Slavic god Radegast located on Mount Radhošť facing the summit. The statue was created by academic sculptor Albin Polasek.
Satina Waterfall near Lysá hora is one of the most famous in this area, with cascades that flow gracefully over rocky outcrops, especially beautiful after rainfall.
